I am a freelance senior researcher, with an extensive experience on European Social policies. With a broad portfolio and versatile competences, I have a deep knowledge in various areas and extensive practice in devising capacity building modules for policy makers.

A graduate in international private and public law, I have been a lecturer at the law department of Nanterre University (Paris X). As former French civil servant, I have extensive experience in labour and employment policies and in gender issues. In Eurofound (the European Agency for the improvement of living and working conditions), I have been responsible for research projects addressing both industrial relations and working conditions developments in Europe.

My main research areas are undeclared work, atypical and fraudulent forms of work, employment status, flexicurity, developments in social partner organisations, working conditions and industrial relations in central public administration. I contributed also to various analyses of the European Working Conditions Survey (EWCS) data, with a focus on gender and employment security issues.

As Italian, born and raised in France, working in Ireland, I feel particularly at ease in European and International environments. I have first-hand knowledge of the challenges but also the great added value, of multicultural settings and discussion.
